# AI Literacy Foundations

AI literacy means understanding what artificial intelligence can do, where it fails, and how to use it responsibly. A beginner should first learn the difference between rule-based automation, machine learning, generative AI, and agentic systems.

Machine learning systems learn patterns from data. Generative AI systems produce text, images, code, audio, or other outputs based on learned patterns. They do not truly understand like a human; they predict useful outputs from context.

Good AI users verify important outputs, provide clear context, protect private data, and know when a human expert should review the result. The goal is not blind trust. The goal is supervised leverage.

Starter exercise: choose one daily task, describe where AI could help, list what information the AI needs, and identify what a human should still verify.
